Pavlova

Pavlova is a sweet, delicate dessert named after Anna Pavlova, a famous Russian ballerina. It is a baked meringue served with whipped cream and fresh fruit or topped with a citrus curd or jam.

Ingredients

  • Whites of 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 3 teaspoons corn startch
  • 3/4 cup homemade whipped cream
  • 2 cups fresh fruit

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 245F
  2. Trace around the bottom of an 8-inch cake pan onto parchment paper
  3. Using a stand or hand mixer, beat egg whites to soft peaks (About 5 minutes)
  4. Gradually add in sugar one tablespoon at a time and beat until mixture is at the stiff peak stage, mixture should be smooth and glossy
  5. Gently fold in cream of tartar and corn startch, careful not to cause the mixture to deflate too much
  6. Place prepared parchment paper onto a baking sheet and spoon the mixture onto the baking tray using the traced circle as a guide
  7. Bake for 1 1/2 hours, then turn oven off and leave the pavlova in the oven for an additional 2 hours
  8. Allow to cool completely and top with whipped cream and fresh fruit before serving
